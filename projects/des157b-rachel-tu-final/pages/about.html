About A Thousand Cranes

SOURCE: Naia Shedd

The kanji characters 千羽鶴 or "senbazuru" translates to 1000 cranes. Though origami has long been a Japanese tradition, the legend surrounding folding 1000 cranes is relatively new. It was popularized after the passing of a girl named Sadako Sasaki, a survivor of the Hiroshima bombing who folded cranes during her battle with Leukemia.
