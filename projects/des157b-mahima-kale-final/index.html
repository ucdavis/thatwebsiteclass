The aim of this website is to start a conversation about race and what a racist idea is. It shows a historical timeline for how the construct of race is woven into the roots of the making of America.


The historical timeline is studied and inspired from the book Stamped from the Beginning, written by Ibram X.Kendi.

  • 933-838 BCE

    The Book of Genesis records an instance of Noah cursing his son Ham's descendants to be slaves. Although there is no biblical evidence that Ham was the "father" of African peoples, various Jewish, Christian and Islamic writers came to believe that he was, and their association helped to justify centuries of African enslavement.


    Medieval curse theorist (Ham theory) laid the foundation for segregationist ideas and racist ideas of black genetic inferiority.


    What aspect of religion plays a role in race or racist idea today?

  • 460-375 BCE

    Hippocrates’s book On Airs, Waters and Places is considered to be the source document of climate-based theories of racial differences. In this treatise, Hippocrates describes the effects of different environments, diets, and customs on human disposition, including physical and moral susceptibilities to disease and humors. These differences are epitomized by European peoples, whom Hippocrates characterizes as ungovernable yet courageous as a result of their cold climate, and Asian peoples, who are characterized as peaceful but timid due to their hot climate.

    Aristotle believed humanity is divided between two - Greeks and the barbarians OR the masters and the slaves.


    He said, "For that some should rule and others be ruled is a thing not only necessary, but expedient; from the hour of their birth, some are marked out for subjection, others for rule."

According to Khaldun, “Sub Saharan Africans [were] submissive to slavery.” At this point, Muslim powers were trading both Africans and Slavic peoples (European ethnolinguistic group) as slaves, yet Khadun’s assimilationist theory helped shift the slave trade toward Africans alone. Khaldun also suggested Hippocrates' climate theory again. He stresses that Africans who migrated to the cooler north were found to produce offsprings who have lighter skin.
